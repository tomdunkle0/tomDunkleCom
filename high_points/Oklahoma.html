Black Mesa (Oklahoma)

Elevation: 4,975ft / 1,516m
Date Completed: April 25, 2019
Chronology: High point #14 of 50

Figure 1: View of the Black Mesa Trail just after ascending to the top of the mesa.

Figure 2: Ry and Tom toast some trail gin-n-tonics in celebration of high-pointing Black Mesa.

The town of Kenton, Oklahoma, near the state's high point of Black Mesa, feels like a true wild western ghost town. We didn't see signs of any inhabitants while passing through, but I believe that there are some.

The Black Mesa is actually a large formation that exists in three different states: Oklahoma, New Mexico, and Colorado. Unsurprisingly, much of the mesa in New Mexico is at a higher elevation than the Oklahoma state high point. There are also several New Mexican mountains visible from Black Mesa that are huge by comparison.

Figure 3: One view of the canyon adjacent to Black Mesa.

Figure 4: Another view of the canyon adjacent to Black Mesa.

The canyon views became impressive as we ascended the mesa. It took just a couple hours' time to summit the mesa and return. The funny part of the climb was getting on top of the mesa and seeing the large monument marking the high point about a half mile away, across what looked like a flat field.

In fact, when we were in the center of the mesa, I almost couldn't tell that I was on top of it. Looking in the distance, the horizon would just show a thin sliver of the canyon around the mesa.

A short trail connected the high point monument to an overlook of Kenton and the surrounding landscape. After we downed our celebratory gin and tonics for completing the high point, we went out to the vantage point to take in the view.
