Driskill Mountain (Louisiana)

Elevation: 535ft / 163m
Date Completed: July 5, 2018
Chronology: High point #6 of 50

Figure 1: The most spectacular view from Driskill Mountain.

Figure 2: The clearing and view point with the Highpointer's Bench visible.

At 535 feet above sea level, Driskill Mountain is the third lowest state high point, behind only Delaware's Ebright Azimuth and Florida's Britton Hill. In contrast to the swamp biomes of southern Louisiana, northern Louisiana is heavily forested, yet still flat.

So far, Driskill Mountain is the only one of the state high points whose summit I've reached on mountain bike rather than on foot. It's an easy ride, and it only took about five minutes.

Figure 3: The Driskill Mountain sign with Tom's mountain bike and gear.

Figure 4: Tom poses in front of the Driskill Mountain sign.

Next to the high point sign is a clearing in the woods that almost provides a scenic view. The trees are just a tad too tall, though. The clearing also features a High Pointer's Bench.

The Driskill Mountain sign also has a tribute to Jack Longacre, the founder of the High Pointer's Club. Jack Longacre's ashes were scattered on Driskill Mountain in 2003.
