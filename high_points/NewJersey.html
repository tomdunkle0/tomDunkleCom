High Point (New Jersey)

Elevation: 1,803ft / 550m
Date Completed: May 23, 2020
Chronology: High point #23 of 50

New Jersey didn't get too creative with naming their state's high point, High Point. The Appalachian Trail passes through High Point State Park, though it doesn't cross the High Point itself. The park is mostly forested, featuring various hiking trails, grotesque millipedes, and bright orange lizards which I refer to as "Jersey Boys".

Figure 3: Plaque marking the High Point of New Jersey.

Figure 4: Tom poses in front of the plaque marking the High Point of New Jersey.

On top of the High Point is a large, towering monument 220 feet tall. The monument sits on top of a square viewing platform. The monument's height makes it visible all around the park, so it's difficult to get lost. The Jersey Boys won't help you if you do.

This was my first post-pandemic high point. I'd driven from Austin, Texas to my hometown of Danbury, Connecticut with a stop to camp in Louisiana, a stop at my parents' house in Georgia, and a stop to camp in Virginia. At the time, New York City was the danger zone for COVID-19, so even just being on hiking trails in New Jersey felt risky.
