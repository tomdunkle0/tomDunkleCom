Mt. Mansfield (Vermont)

Elevation: 4,395ft / 1,340m
Date Completed: October 13, 2018
Chronology: High point #11 of 50

Figure 1: The summit of Mt. Mansfield, with the trail visible.

Figure 2: The geographical marker at the Mt. Mansfield summit.

Mt. Mansfield is a relatively steep climb, but it doesn't last long. It took us just a couple of hours to summit the mountain and return. Despite doing this late in the season, we had a day with clear weather and it was perfect. The snow covering the upper half of the mountain contrasts with the fall colors in the trees below the snow line.

The summit of Mt. Mansfield is a rocky dome that's covered in snow, albeit with a marked trail crossing it. The summit can be reached from two trails.

The clouds had a way of continually engulfing the summit, and this created a dynamic viewing experience as certain places disappeared in the clouds while others come into view.

Figure 6: A clear lake on the ridgeline of Mt. Mansfield, as seen from near the summit of Mt. Mansfield.

Figure 7: A different view of the lake.

Just like when conquering New York's Mt. Marcy the day before, there was a unique contrast of seasons on Mt. Mansfield. Most of the mountain was above the 'season line' and therefore covered in snow, but the forests at the base of the mountain and in the surrounding land showed the familiar warmer colors of a New England autumn.

Several parts of Mt. Mansfield are named after body parts, implying that the mountain itself resembles a human face. The summit is called "The Chin", and "The Adam's Apple" is a protruding feature slightly lower along one of the mountain's ridges. Just below the Adam's Apple is the Lake of the Clouds, as seen in Figures 6 and 7.
