Charles Mound (Illinois)

Elevation: 1,235ft / 376m
Date Completed: June 1, 2019
Chronology: High point #17 of 50

Figure 1: Sign at Charles Mound, near the geographical marker.

Figure 2: Geographical marker at Charles Mound.

Illinois' Charles Mound is perhaps the high point that is most invasive to its property owners. The geographical marker for the point itself is only about 30 feet in front of the front door of their house, and it's a large farm property so you have to hike a mile or so to get to the high point. Although not open to the public year-round, they were nice enough to open it on the weekends during the summer in 2019 when we visited.

Figure 3: Tom and Ry pose at the sign on top of Charles Mound.

Figure 4: A hill adjacent to the high point at Charles Mound.

The high point also has a sign marking it, and a couple of chairs set up to enjoy the view. Since it was only open during summer weekends, there were quite a few people along the trail--I'd say we saw maybe 20 others on the way up and down.

Figure 5: A gross pond along the road to Charles Mound.

Figure 6: A different, equally gross pond along the road to Charles Mound.

Rather than using their driveway to get there, there's a separate hiking trail across the property that you can access by parking along the street at a marked location. The trail runs alongside an unpaved road, then up through some fields alongside a few really swampy ponds. Since we visited in early June, it was buggy and humid out. This high pointing trip was the one where we made heavy use of bug nets and hats.

Figure 7: The Chicago apartment at which the weekend of highpointing began.

Figure 8: The Jeep that we rented, primarily to drive the roads near Mt. Arvon in Michigan.

For me, this high point was the first in a blitzkrieg of four: Illinois, Wisconsin, Minnesota, and Michigan. Because accessing the Michigan high point required more than just a sedan, Ry and I had rented a brand new 2019 Jeep Wrangler with about 200 miles on it. We finished the trip with over 2,500. The downside was that after flying into Chicago and picking up the Jeep, I had to find a place to park it in Evanston, the Chicago neighborhood where Ry's sister Haley and her boyfriend lived at the time.
