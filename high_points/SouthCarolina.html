Sassafras Mountain (South Carolina)

Elevation: 3,560ft / 1,085m
Date Completed: December 24, 2019
Chronology: High point #22 of 50

Figure 1: Sassafras Mountain, as viewed from the trail while approaching.

Figure 2: Layers of clouds collecting in the foothills of Sassafras Mountain.

Sassafras Mountain's peak is right along the border between North and South Carolina. It's possible to drive to the top, or you can hike from a variety of distances. When I visited, I started at the Foothills Trail entrance A4. The trail is moderately steep, and covers picturesque South Carolina wilderness.

Figure 3: Tom poses on the summit of Sassafras Mountain.

Figure 4: Tom poses with the High Pointer's Bench on the summit of Sassafras Mountain.

Various portions of the trail reminded me of the shape of the roads along Iceland's fjords. The way that the trail weaves in and out of the mountainside is similar in shape to how roads are cut along the latter country's coastlines.

Figure 5: A section of the Sassafras Mountain trail cut into the hillside.

Figure 6: A different section of the Sassafras Mountain trail cut into the hillside.

From the summit of the mountain, I saw thick clouds of fog sitting in the valleys between the foothills. This is a phenomenon I've seen in several parts of Appalachia over the years. Now that I'm looking at the photos again, I guess that I've never had the reverse experience of being in one of these clouds of fog.

Figure 7: Foothills of Sassafras Mountain.

Figure 8: Foothills of Sassafras Mountain with layers of clouds visible to one side, and a sidewalk as part of the summit architecture on the other side.

The summit has a large concrete viewing platform built on top of it. On top of the platform is the marked border between the two Carolinas (see Figure 15 at the bottom of the page). Near the viewing platform is a series of paved walkways leading down to the Foothills Trail, as well as a network of large rocks that you can wander around.

Figure 9: Walkways and rock features near the Sassafras Mountain summit, as seen from the summit itself.

Figure 10: Walkways near the Sassafras Mountain summit, as seen while approaching from the trail.

The trail's pretty well maintained. I think there are many more miles of trail in this area beyond what I touched on Sassafras, and I'm sure it's a great place to spend time. I remember feeling constrained that I had to take a flight back to Texas just a couple of days later, because I wanted to do a longer camping trip in this area. I probably will sometime in the future.

Figure 11: Forests and foothills of Sassafras Mountain, as seen from the summit.

Towards the end of the hike, there were long shadows cast through the woods. This is both because of the shape of the hills, and the angle of the sun due to how late in the year it was (only three days after the winter equinox). Despite being Christmas Eve, it was incredibly warm out and I was sweating. I forget exactly but I think it was in the 60s F.
