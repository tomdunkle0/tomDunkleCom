Cheaha Mountain (Alabama)

Elevation: 2,413ft / 735m
Date Completed: November 30, 2021
Chronology: High point #36 of 50

Figure 1: The Talladega National Forest, engulfed in smoke, as seen from the Cheaha Mountain observation tower.

Figure 2: Tom poses in front of the sign marking Alabama's highest point.

During Thanksgiving week in 2021, I drove to visit my parents in Georgia. I was planning to stop at Cheaha Mountain on the way, but was unable to enter the state park on my first attempt (Nov. 21) due to the ongoing Duck Nest and Fall Branch wildfires. As I drove into the park on route 281, I saw a couple of crews of firefighters, and then found that the park was closed. So, I kept my eye on the news and learned after a few days that the park had opened up again. I visited on the 30th, on my way back to Texas.

Figure 3: The observation tower, plus various signs and a statue at the peak of Cheaha Mountain.

Figure 4: The sign marking Alabama's highest point.

There's a large network of trails around Cheaha Mountain, and I want to take a longer backpacking approach sometime, because you can stretch it out into 20+ miles if you really want to. On this day, however, I was in a bit of a hurry and only had time to drive right up to the top, which is also an option.

Figures 5-8: The stairwell, front door, and telescope inside the observation tower on Cheaha Mountain.

At the summit is a large stone observation tower. A spiral stairwell leads up to the viewing area, which has glass windows that slide open and closed. There's also a telescope for viewing at a distance. I didn't realize at first, but when I looked in the correct direction I spotted one of the wildfires, still burning. In fact, the news had said that the wildfires were 90% contained on the day that I visited the park. What I saw was the remaining 10%.
