Spruce Knob (West Virginia)

Elevation: 4,863ft / 1,482m
Date Completed: May 25, 2020
Chronology: High point #26 of 50

Figure 1: A view of some Appalachian pines from the trail near the summit of Spruce Knob.

Figure 2: A view of the Appalachians from the trail near the summit of Spruce Knob.

West Virginia's high point, Spruce Knob, is located in a picturesque stretch of the Appalachian mountains. The mountains are covered with pine forests, and show a nice combination of green and brown in the summertime.

Figure 3: Tom points at the geographic marker at the Spruce Knob summit.

Figure 4: West Virginian landscape and pines near the summit of Spruce Knob.

The summit is easily accessible, with a parking lot nearby and a small network of hiking trails around it, plus an observation tower next to the high point. We also went downwards along a nearby trail just to gain some mileage.

While exploring near the summit, we saw some guys paragliding which was pretty amazing. They jumped from a nearby peak and sat in the sky for a while, just floating around.

Figure 5: A mossy stretch of ground next to the trail to Spruce Knob.

Figure 6: A naturally occurring Appalachian car air freshener forest, as seen near the trail to Spruce Knob.

There were a lot of different mosses growing all over the ground. There were several places in the woods where people had set up makeshift 'couches' and loveseats out of large rock slabs, and then set them around fire pits. At one point, we saw a naturally occurring forest of tiny little ~6-inch tall pine trees. I was delighted to finally understand where car air fresheners come from.

Figure 7: Another view of the Appalachians from near the summit of Spruce Knob.

Figure 8: A farm in West Virginia, near Spruce Knob.

We really liked the campsite we'd set up the first night in West Virginia, near the Swallow Rock Campground. The only downside of it was an arguably dangerous bridge that we had to cross each time any of us walked between camp and the cars in the parking lot. I think we're all just happy to have checked off this high point without being killed by that bridge.

...or by the bear that presumably wandered through the campsite during the day, which we found some pretty clear 'evidence' of. We packed up the campsite and moved to a different one a couple of miles away.
