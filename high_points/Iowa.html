Hawkeye Point (Iowa)

Elevation: 1,670ft / 510m
Date Completed: October 16, 2022
Chronology: High point #39 of 50

Figure 1: Hawkeye Point, the highest natural point in Iowa.

Figure 2: Tom, Ry, and Victoria posing at Hawkeye Point.

Hawkeye Point is one of the most geographically isolated state high points. We had to spend about ten hours driving through cornfields to get there. The site features several monuments--benches, a mosaic, plaques, signs pointing to almost every other state high point, and an information board with a guest register.

Figure 3: The cornfield adjacent to Hawkeye Point.

Figure 4: The cornfield adjacent to Hawkeye Point, at sunset.

Just like everything in Iowa that isn't a cornfield, Hawkeye Point is situated next to a cornfield. We were racing the sun to get there before dark. We did make it in time to see the high point during the day, and to catch the sunset. We also played a game of corn hacky sack with some corn cobs.

Figure 5: Signs pointing to other state high points.

Figure 6: More signs pointing to other state high points.

There are four posts holding up signs pointing to 39 of the 49 other state high points. The distances are listed in miles, giving you a sense of how far away you are from all of them.

Figure 7: The mosaic platform at Hawkeye Point.

Figure 8: The information board at Hawkeye Point.

It was cold and windy, and we had to continue driving to Omaha, Nebraska so that I could check into a hotel to work from the next day. So, we didn't stick around long, but we checked this one off!
