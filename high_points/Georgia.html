Brasstown Bald (Georgia)

Elevation: 4,784ft / 1,458m
Date Completed: November 29, 2019
Chronology: High point #21 of 50

Figure 1: The ridgeline of Brasstown Bald, as viewed from the trail while approaching.

Figure 2: The foothills of Brasstown Bald, with Lake Hiawassee visible in the background.

Georgia's Brasstown Bald is located in the mountainous Appalachian region in the north of the state. The mountain can be summited on foot from a few different directions, or you can drive to parking lot that's a short hike from the summit, but personally I don't like to live that way. No disrespect if you do.

I climbed this mountain with my brother Bill, and we started in the town of Young Harris, at the trailhead on Bald Mountain Road. We descended the mountain via the Arkaquah Trailhead. The whole journey took about six hours, so it's a hike but it's nothing too crazy.

Figure 3: View of the parking lot from the summit of Brasstown Bald.

Figure 4: Tom and Bill pose in front of the sign explaining the history of the name Brasstown Bald.

There's a pretty large building and observation deck at the summit of the mountain. Since I visited during the COVID-19 pandemic, the facility was closed but people were still able to access the deck. The deck provided views of all of the surrounding landmarks with the exception of occasional cloud obstruction.

Figure 5: Looking up towards the trail leading to the ridge of Brasstown Bald.

Figure 6: Looking down towards the trail leading to the parking lot in Young Harris.

The trail was mostly covered in leaves, being that it was November and the trees had finished shedding. It's also rural Georgia, so the air is alive with gunshots from every direction in the distance. We didn't see much at all in the way of wildlife, and that's probably a good thing because I'd be concerned about running into a bear here.

Figures 7-9: Several views of Young Harris from along the trail.

On the way down the mountain, we hiked along a ridgeline with the town of Young Harris to our right the whole way down. This provided many interesting views of the town.

Figure 10: A view from the summit of Brasstown Bald, showing the thick cloud coverage.

Figure 11: Thick cloud coverage viewed horizontally over a railing on the vacant visitor's center at the summit of Brasstown Bald.

The overcast weather that we had that day gave the mountains an almost purple tint. As the clouds danced around, different parts of the landscape were periodically hidden from view. At times, everything below us disappeared.
