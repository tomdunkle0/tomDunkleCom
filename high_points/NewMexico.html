Wheeler Peak (New Mexico)

Elevation: 13,167ft / 4,013m
Date Completed: April 24, 2019
Chronology: High point #13 of 50

Figure 1: Plaque honoring George Wheeler at the summit of Wheeler Peak.

Figure 2: View from the summit with our trekking poles visible.

As of the current writing (31/50 high points), I consider New Mexico's Wheeler Peak to be both the most difficult and my favorite personal high point that I've achieved. I've had to process more pain in climbing this mountain than any other--both during and after the climb. For that reason, I think I've learned the most from it.

Figure 3: First person view of the Sangre de Cristo mountains and my snowshoes.

Figure 4: Ry sits down to take a break while we trek downwards along the snowshoe-print tracks we created to get to the summit.

Right after we passed Williams Lake, we came to a hill in the woods and suddenly the trail got steep. Really, really steep. Then, tree line abruptly occurred, leaving us to continue climbing upwards slowly for about a mile. We spent this mile on steep, exposed and snowy slopes, moving by kicking little 'stairs' into the mountainside with our snowshoes.

We were also struggling to stay on the trail using a digital map, since the trail itself was submerged in snow. So, we sort of invented a path that went back and forth along the mountainside until we reached the top. It was painfully slow work, but finally we reached a ledge where I took the photo in Figure 3, which is likely my favorite photograph I've ever taken. From that point, it was pretty smooth sailing to the top. On the way, we passed New Mexico's highest mountain goat.

Figure 5: Downward view of some of the snow staircases that we created.

Figure 6: Upward view of some of the snow staircases that we created.

The one good thing about having to kick all of those staircases into the snow was that when descending we actually knew where we were going. Still, it was hard work and by the time we were back down at Williams Lake, I remember feeling that my body was in a state of physical exhaustion beyond most if not absolutely anything I'd pushed it through before.

We also both got gnarly sunburns--mine the worst I've ever had. Huge moral here, if you are climbing a mountain covered in snow be absolutely sure you have not just sunscreen on but fabric protection of everything including your face. I went through quite a bit of lagging pain after I finished this high point.
