Timms Hill (Wisconsin)

Elevation: 1,951ft / 595m
Date Completed: June 1, 2019
Chronology: High point #18 of 50

Figure 1: The Hill of Beans during the daytime, as viewed from the watchtower.

Figure 2: The Hill of Beans at sunset, as viewed from the Swimming Beach.

Out of all of the state high points, Wisconsin's definitely wins the award for best adjacent restaurant. The Hill of Beans is a bed and breakfast with great t-shirts and even better food, and it sits just on the other side of Bass Lake from Timms Hill, the state high point.

Figure 3: The base of the observation tower at Timms Hill.

Figure 4: The Timms Hill register, seen at the bottom of the observation tower's staircase.

There's an ~two-story tall wooden observation tower at the summit of Timms Hill, with a high pointer's register mailbox at the base. Not far from here is the obligatory High Pointer's Bench. The observation tower provides a 360 degree view of the lush, green central Wisconsin landscape surrounding what amounts to little more than a hill of beans.

Figure 5: The High Pointer's Bench at Timms Hill.

Figure 6: Tom and Ry pose on top of the observation tower at Timms Hill.

Flat as it may be, Wisconsin has some really nice forest coverage and it's a great place to be outdoors in the summer. If I lived here I would probably be more into lake activities (boating, fishing, kayaking, etc.) than I am.

I got a kick out of the fact that one of the most popular gas station chains is called Krist. We kept joking about searching for Krist whenever we started running low on gas.

Figures 7-9: Several views of the central Wisconsin landscape from the observation deck on top of Timms Hill.

As cultured of a district as Timms Hill appears to be, it is not without its troublemakers. A helpful and informative sign warned us that bringing horses onto the swimming beach was strictly forbidden, and that anybody who broke the rule risked the communal consequence of getting horses banned from the land established around this hill of beans.

If I were in my homeland, I'd go down fighting for the right to bring my horse to the swimming beach. But I was a guest in this land, and I wasn't going to question their lifestyles.

Figure 10: A lake near Timms Hill at sunrise.

Figure 11: The Swimming Beach, at sunset. Horses are strictly forbidden.

There's a strong technicality on the date I've logged for summitting this high point. Having left Chicago on the morning of June 1st and made a quick stop at Charles Mound (the Illinois state high point), we arrived at Timms Hill late in the evening and ventured onto it out of curiosity before coming back and setting up camp for the night. We then returned in the morning to take more daylight photographs. So, the majority of the photos you see here are from June 2nd, but June 1st was the day we truly summited the high point for the first time.
