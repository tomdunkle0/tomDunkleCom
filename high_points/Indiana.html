Hoosier Hill (Indiana)

Elevation: 1,257ft / 383m
Date Completed: November 21, 2020
Chronology: High point #31 of 50

Figure 1: The register and picnic table at Hoosier Hill.

Figure 2: The stone High Point monument at Hoosier Hill.

Similar to Kansas and Illinois, Indiana's state high point is located on private land owned by folks nice enough to open it up to the public. The high point is located in a small wooded patch just across the road from their house, and there's a dirt and gravel road established for visitors to park their cars.

Figure 3: Our cars parked just outside the entrance to Hoosier Hill.

Figure 4: Tom and Ry pose with the Indiana High Point Register.

There's a short little trail that appears to lead somewhere into the woods, but it quickly fades into an undefined woods, and the whole area's small enough that you can tell there's not much else to discover. Still, definitely a nice little enclave.

Figure 5: The short trail at Hoosier Hill leading to essentially nowhere.

Figure 6: Another view of the woods at Hoosier Hill.

As with many of the more easily accessible high points, there's a High Pointer's Bench at Hoosier Hill. But, this one's different from the others because an animal appears to have been gnawing on a corner of it. Or maybe some foolish kid was sharpening a knife on it? Either way, somebody's been having their way with this bench.

Figure 7: The High Pointer's bench at Hoosier Hill.

Figure 8: The evident gnawing marks on the High Pointer's bench at Hoosier Hill.

After checking off the Indiana state high point, we once again retreated into Ohio to spend the night at the Arrowhead Campground. The funny thing about camping in the midwest close to winter is that not only do established campsites all shut down, but nobody's even there to monitor them so you can just go camp as you please. We claimed a really sweet spot in the woods.

Having already had a somewhat miserable morning waking up in puddles of mushy, damp ground while getting rained on, we spent the whole evening grimacing over what we expected the following morning to be like. But it was fine. In the morning, Ry got in his car and left for Colorado for the ski season while I got in mine and left for northern Georgia to visit my parents.
