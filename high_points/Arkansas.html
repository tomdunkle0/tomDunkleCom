Mount Magazine (Arkansas)

Elevation: 2,753ft / 839m
Date Completed: July 7, 2018
Chronology: High point #8 of 50

Figure 1: Panorama of the view from along Mt. Magazine Road at the entrance to the State Park.

Arkansas' Mount Magazine is a wide one, with enough height to produce the view in the panorama above, yet enough breadth for that vantage point to be removed from the true summit, which is deemed Signal Hill. It's a very short hike from the viewpoint to the top, though, and this is one of those mountains where it's up to you how much distance you want to cover when you climb, or 'climb' it. If you want to just see the top, drive to the parking lot and walk five minutes. Or you can take several miles to get there on various trails.

Figure 2: Signal Hill, the true peak of Mount Magazine.

Figure 3: Tom poses with the high point sign at Signal Hill.

Signal Hill is a clearing in the woods that is marked with a large sign. On the ground is an impressive stone representation of the state of Arkansas, including a mark showing the location of Mount Magazine in the state. Signal Hill can be reached via several different trails in the park's network.

Figure 4: A view of the northwestern Arkansas landscape from Mt. Magazine Road.

Figure 5: A different view of the northwestern Arkansas landscape from Mt. Magazine Road.

The summit of the mountain has both a campground and a fancy three-story lodge, so if you want to stick it out up there you have your choice of amenities. If I had more time to explore this area, I wouldn't mind camping up there, or renting a room in the lodge and pitching a tent in the hallway just to be ironic.

I say this, though, and coincidentally the area around Mount Magazine has become a regular step for me, because I have camped at the nearby Cove Lake several times while travelling distances via car during the pandemic. Would highly recommend.
