Black Mountain (Kentucky)

Elevation: 4,145ft / 1,263m
Date Completed: July 6, 2017
Chronology: High point #4 of 50

Figure 1: Part of Black Mountain, and the distant Kentucky landscape, as viewed from KY Route 160.

Figure 2: A sign marking Black Mountain along KY Route 160.

Black Mountain, the highest peak in Kentucky, is right next to the state border between Kentucky and Virginia. The mountain is unique among high points in that there is no clear way to climb it on foot. There's no established hiking trails on it that I'm aware of, and the forest on and around it is dense enough that you don't want to bushwhack it, unless you're really looking for an adventure.

However, Kentucky state route 160 traverses the mountain and into Virginia, and the road can be used to access the summit. Near the summit, there's a dirt road called Black Mountain Ridge Road that connects route 160 to the mountain's summit. If you don't want to drive all the way up, you can park at the bottom of Black Mountain Ridge Road and walk up to the top (a little over a mile each way).

Figure 3: The FAA radar dome near the Black Mountain summit in clear weather.

Figure 4: The FAA radar dome near the Black Mountain summit in overcast weather.

The summit of the mountain is vaguely accessible. There's a bunch of equipment that is maintained and used by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), including a large, ominous-looking radar dome. Much of the premises is fenced off and covered in warning signs telling you not to enter. However, I believe the summit itself is open to the public as long as you don't try to wander into any buildings or over any fences.

There's a plaque at the top honoring William Risden, a cable TV innovator that did much of his work around Black Mountain. The area is densely forested, and like most of Kentucky it's continually raining and never feels dry. We found some bright orange lizards several times, one of which is pictured in Figure 7.

Sometime in the future, I want to come back and see if I can figure out a way to properly climb this mountain on foot, though doing so is low on my list.
