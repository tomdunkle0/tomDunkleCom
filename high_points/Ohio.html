Campbell Hill (Ohio)

Elevation: 1,549ft / 472m
Date Completed: November 21, 2020
Chronology: High point #30 of 50

Figure 1: The high point facility at the naively-named Ohio Hi-Point Career Center.

Figure 2: Helpful signs mark the high point, and make you aware that you are being watched and that you damn well better not sled.

Ohio's Campbell Hill has a rich military history, as it used to be an air defense station. Nowadays it's not only open to the public, but also a community college. One of the more interesting features is a rotating satellite dish. It seems very technical, but alas there are people that don't want you to climb it. Signs warn that you're on camera, and there's an array of bricks in the ground honoring people that the monument claims to be war veterans. However, I suspect that the bricks truly represent folks who've died climbing on the rotating satellite dish. I have a talent for reading between the lines.

I visited this place during a rainy pre-Thanksgiving weekend in 2020. While driving from Austin, Texas to visit my parents in Blairsville, Georgia, I took a detour north and met up with Ry so that we could visit the Ohio and Indiana state high points.

Figure 3: A historical sign at the high point documents Charles Campbell's ownership of the land and its military history.

Figure 4: Tom and Ry pose in front of the High Point sign.

In the game of high-pointing, you just have to attack them when you can. It was a cold and dreary weekend, but it was fun. Before visiting Campbell Hill, we spent the night camping on Kiser Lake. Ry & I were the only two people there, which honestly surprised me because I figured more people would be in the boat we were in--travelling for the holidays but trying to be ultra-cautious about social distancing and not carrying disease.

Figure 5: Tom's car and the campsite at Kiser Lake on a rainy morning.

Figure 6: The picnic table at our campsite on a rainy morning.

Kiser Lake is a nice campground, even if we didn't get to experience it in the best conditions. There's a large number of sites and the lake is serene. I showed up and started setting up camp around 8pm, and Ry arrived a couple of hours later as he was driving from Connecticut to Colorado. Initially there was a cat that was fishing in the lake, and it started to creep around the site while I was setting up. But, the cat disappeared after a little while.

And then, so did we.
