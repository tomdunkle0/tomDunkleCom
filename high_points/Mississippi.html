Woodall Mountain (Mississippi)

Elevation: 807ft / 246m
Date Completed: July 6, 2018
Chronology: High point #7 of 50

Figure 1: The register and High Pointer's bench at the summit of Woodall Mountain.

Figure 2: Tom poses with the register at the summit of Woodall Mountain.

Mississippi's Woodall Mountain is a rural scene in the northeastern corner of the state. I took the Natchez Trace Parkway there all the way from Jackson. The summit features a High Pointer's Bench, a mailbox register, and a couple of nondescript telecomms buildings. There is also a plaque discussing the history of the mountain (see Figure 5 at the bottom of the page).

As the name suggests, Woodall Mountain Road leads to the summit of Woodall Mountain. To keep things fresh and interesting, I did an inverted climb of the mountain. I drove my car to the top, parked it, walked down to the bottom, and then back up.

Figure 3: Low view of Woodall Mountain road.

Figure 4: High view of Woodall Mountain road.

Woodall Mountain Road is a dirt road, and there are a few little driveways off of it. One of the driveways was, according to a posted sign, for a local hunting club. The area is peaceful and quiet, and I don't think I saw anybody else there. The road gets steep for just a couple hundred feet near the end, but it's a pretty low grade up until then.
