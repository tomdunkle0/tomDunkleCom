Guadalupe Peak (Texas)

Elevation: 8,751ft / 2,667m
Date Completed: April 9, 2016
Chronology: High point #2 of 50

Figure 1: View of El Capitan from the summit of Guadalupe Peak.

Figure 2: The monument at the summit of Guadalupe Peak, along with a homemade monument we temporarily erected.

The Guadalupe Peak trip was described by Ry as "the most disgusting thing I've ever done with my body", in reference to the multiple layers of sunscreen and grime we all acquired over several days. Even in early April, the Guadalupe Mountains are engulfed in a blazing, sunny heat and the only brief salvation is during the night.

The mountain is right next to El Capitan, a summit that is lower but perhaps more visually spectacular. When you get up to the summit of Guadalupe, you get some nice overhead views of El Capitan in front of the Chihuahan Desert. There is also a trail that goes to the top of El Capitan, but unfortunately I have still never had a chance to climb it.

Figure 3: The Chihuahan Desert, as viewed from about a third of the way up the Guadalupe Peak trail.

Figure 4: From left to right: Yiran, Ry, Tom, Luke.

It's so cool how the Guadalupe Mountains contrast with the adjacent Chihuahan Desert. The desert is flat, colorful, and expansive. It looks like an ocean of sand that gets larger the more altitude you gain on the mountain. Many of the hills and curves even look like stationary 'waves' in the ground.

The day before we climbed Guadalupe Peak, we climbed Hunter Peak as a warm-up, and then we came down through Pine Springs Canyon on the Tejas Trail. That descent is one that I highly recommend.

Figure 5: The Chihuahan Desert, as seen from near the summit of Guadalupe Peak.

Figure 6: Neighboring peaks in the Guadalupe Mountains, as seen from near the summit of Guadalupe Peak.

The summit has a large, relatively flat area that a lot of people can walk around on. It was windy the day that we were there, and that made it difficult for us to fly the flag we'd created with a very important message for my friend A.J. (he's one of the ones who originally convinced me to start this whole journey, see the New Hampshire post for more context).
