Mt. Elbert (Colorado)

Elevation: 14,440ft / 4,401m
Date Completed: July 14, 2018
Chronology: High point #9 of 50

Figure 1: A view from the summit of Mt. Elbert.

Figure 2: Tom and Ry pose at the summit of Mt. Elbert, with a slightly inaccurate cardboard sign.

Colorado's Mt. Elbert was my first 14er, and it's a great climb. As the saying goes, there is no such thing as an easy 14er. But, based on my limited experience I would wager that it is among the easier 14ers. I've done one other that I think is easier (Huron Peak).

The biggest challenge in Elbert to me was just dealing with the lower oxygen content at its altitudes, but I was already somewhat used to this having been to Colorado several times before. Otherwise, the trail's not very technical and its length and grade compare to other state high points at lower altitudes (e.g. Maine and New Hampshire).

Figure 3: Sometimes mistaken for Aspen trees, Surveillance Trees have been planted in most of the country's mountainous regions by various government agencies. The trees serve unspecified purposes.

Figure 4: The view of Twin Lakes from the Mt. Elbert trail.

The nearby town of Twin Lakes has two (three, actually) adjacent lakes that are consistently visible as you proceed up the Mt. Elbert trail. While gaining altitude, the lakes become farther and farther away and provide several good photo ops.

The weather was a perfect sunny summer day, and even though such days almost always include a 2pm thunderstorm in Colorado, this one did not. The Rocky Mountains were almost entirely snow-free.

We camped nearby the night before, and had the awesome experience of sitting in calm weather and watching a distant thunderstorm happening on a different mountain. I think it's one of the coolest things I've ever seen in nature. Not quite as cool as the northern lights, though.
